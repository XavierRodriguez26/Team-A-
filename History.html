History of Autonomous Vehicles

Self-Propelled Car

Leonardo DaVinci - 1487

The Self Propelled Car was a design created by Leonardo Davinci. Designed for festival use, the design was never fully invented, and simply stayed as a blueprint. It could be set to either go straight, or move at preset angles.

Whitehead Torpedo

Robert Whitehead - 1866

The Whitehead Torpedo was the first ever self-propelled torpedo to be developed. The torpedo's design was perfected in 1866 by Robert Whitehead from a rough draft design created by Giovanni Luppis. It was driven by three cylinder compressed-air engines. This torpedo was implemented into several different Navy services during the 1970's.

Radio Controlled Car

Francis Houdina - 1925

The Radio Controlled Car was a radio operated car that used an antenna, and had a second car to follow as a transmitter for it. The Radio signals it used operated small electric motors that controlled the movement of the car. Houdina publically demonstrated this car in New York City.

Stanford Cart

Stanford - 1961

The Stanford Cart was the first design to actually be brought to life. The cart could navigate using cameras and an early beta version of an AI. The Stanford Cart went through 43 years of development, and ups and downs. The cart first became developed under the hands of a Mechanical Engineering Graduate student names James L. Adams.

Toyota Automated Parallel Parking

Toyota Automotive - 2003

Toyota first implemented thait Automated Parallel Parking assistance feature in 2003. This allowed for the use of a digital screen located on the center of your dashbaord.

Waymo

Waymo -3008

Waymo was founded as a company in 2008, which unveiled their plan for the Pribot, its first autonomous vehicle. The Company had sued Uber, which gave the company the startup fund for the project they would unveil in 2017, a level 4 Autonomous Van, which came with the unveiling of their new sensors and chips which were said to be cheaper, and cameras that improve visibility.
